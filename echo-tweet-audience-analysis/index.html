Echo: Twitter Audience Analysis

node.js • Aug 15, 2022

Is there anybody out there?

It’s a slow Friday. You finish in 30 minutes and can almost taste the cold pint waiting for you at the local. You have already agreed to go out for after-work drinks, and now begins the chess-like game of which coworker will suggest we leave first.

You still have to reply to that client that keeps chasing you, and you could do a few bits to get a head start on Monday, but that’s a “Monday you” problem. “Friday you” has no care for “Monday you”. “Friday you” would push “Monday you” in front of a bus if it meant you got to the bar quicker.

You decide to fill your time with some social media. So you write the perfect tweet, a perfect balance of wit and humour, hit send, and head to the pub.

On Saturday, you wake up and check your Twitter. 0 likes, 0 retweets.

Why?

Just like slipping out to the pub on a Friday, timing is everything; it’s simple. You have tweeted at the wrong time for your followers; they are not scrolling while you are tweeting.

But how do you know when your users are online? When is the best time to tweet?

We can identify when the users are online in four easy steps:

  1. Get a list of your followers
  2. Get a list of people you are following.
  3. When they tweet (when they are online).
  4. Create a visualisation that shows when to post

Even with the multiple timezones of my user base, you can identify some spikes in the week where my users are most active. Posting at these times ensures that I am posting at the times when my users are using the platform.
