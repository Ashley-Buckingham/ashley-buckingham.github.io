Why AI will not replace developers

Dec 23, 2022

AI has recently been thrown around as a 'buzz term'. In the last year, it seemed as if any company with the most basic of  logic was plastered with one or more of words 'AI', 'Artificial intelligence or 'real AI.'

While the true definition of AI is up for debate, companies are developing complex deep learning. One such company OpenAI has developed GPT-3. GPT-3, as described by its authors, is an 'autoregressive language model that uses deep learning to produce human-like text'.

In the last few months, we have seen OpenAI use this model as a basis for many products and showcases. For example, you may be familiar with GitHub co-pilot, a code completion suggestion tool if you are a developer. If you are an artist, you may be familiar with regenerative art or other products such as DAL-E, also created by OpenAI, which generates digital images from natural language descriptions.

On November 30, 2022, OpenAI released its latest beta, ChatGPT. ChatGPT uses a GPT-3 algorithm to create a chatbot that can hold context between prompts and can be used for many things, such as explaining complex theories,  writing articles and writing code. It is the latter example that caused me to ask the question, "will AI replace the need for developers"?

Instead of creating a post outlining and defending the need for developers in a world of AI, why not get ChatGPT to do it for me? The following are the prompts I used to create the post.

Generating LinkedIn Post

Write a thought-provoking article about why we still need developers when we have tools like chatGPT. Make it a size that will fit in a LinkedIn post

Despite the impressive capabilities of tools like chatGPT, which uses artificial intelligence to generate human-like text, there are several reasons why we will still need developers in the tech industry.

While chatGPT and similar tools can generate coherent text, they are not capable of creative problem-solving or out-of-the-box thinking in the way that human developers are. They also lack the ability to understand the nuances and complexities of human language and are not able to adapt to new technologies and environments. Additionally, developers bring valuable skills and responsibilities such as testing and debugging software, collaborating with team members, and working with customers and clients, which chatGPT and other AI-powered tools are not capable of. In conclusion, developers will continue to be an essential part of the tech industry, even with the existence of chatGPT and similar technologies.

Make the main arguments into bullet points.

Despite the impressive capabilities of tools like chatGPT, they are not capable of creative problem-solving or out-of-the-box thinking in the way that human developers are.

ChatGPT and similar tools are not able to fully grasp the subtleties of language or the context in which certain words and phrases are used.

ChatGPT and similar tools are not able to learn and adapt to new programming languages, frameworks, and platforms in the same way that human developers can.

Developers bring valuable skills and responsibilities such as testing and debugging software, collaborating with team members, and working with customers and clients, which chatGPT and other AI-powered tools are not capable of.

Add a catchy aggressive one-line introduction.

ChatGPT may be able to generate human-like text, but it can never replace the creativity, adaptability, and human understanding of a developer.

Perfect... A post was generated in a matter of minutes to defend developers worldwide.
