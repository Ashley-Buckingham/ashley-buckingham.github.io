NFT Viewer

Jan 22, 2023

The NFT viewer updates in realtime to show the latest artwork being added to the Solona blockchain. Hover on picture for details.

It's 2022, and the world has gone cryptocurrency crazy. Everyone from friends in the pub to colleagues in the boardroom talked about cryptocurrency, blockchain, and various dogs going to the moon.

Then came the flood of NFTs. Non-fungible tokens (NFTs) are used to represent digital items such as artwork, music, videos, and other types of digital content. Because each NFT is unique, it can't be replaced or exchanged for something else, like a traditional digital file can be. This makes them valuable, and they can be bought and sold like other types of assets. When you purchase an NFT, you are buying the rights to that specific digital item and you can prove your ownership via the blockchain.

A blockchain is a digital ledger that records transactions across a network of computers. It's called a "blockchain" because it is made up of blocks of data that are linked together in a chain. Each block contains a record of multiple transactions, and once a block is added to the chain, it cannot be altered. The biggest advantage of using a blockchain is that it is decentralized, meaning that it is not controlled by a single entity, it is open-source and maintained by a network of computers around the world, this makes it more secure and less susceptible to fraud.

While the use and success of NFT are highly debatable, I enjoy looking at the unique and beautiful artwork being created and added to the blockchain every second. This is why I created the NFT viewer displayed at the top of the article.

How does it work (Tech Bit)?

Step 1 - Choosing a blockchain
NFTs are built on blockchain technology, a digital ledger that records transactions securely and transparently. There are many different blockchains available, but I choose Solana.

One of the most significant advantages of Solana for NFTs is its fast transaction speeds. Solana can process up to 65,000 transactions per second, which is much faster than other popular blockchain platforms like Ethereum. This means that buying and selling NFTs on Solana can be done quickly and with minimal delays.


Step 2 - Connecting to the blockchain.

To connect to the blockchain, I used quickNode. This allows me to query the Solana blockchain without hosting a node myself.

Step 3 - Getting the NFT from the blockchain

This part was tricky, and there were a few hoops I had to jump through. The result was an application that followed the steps below:

a. Get the latest block from the blockchain.

b. Loop through all the transactions and filter the ones that have amount=1 (NFTs are unique and therefore show as a maximum of 1)

c. Get the mint Public key from the transaction.

d. Pass the key to metaplex to get the metadata of the transaction

e. Fetch the content of the URL found in the metadata. This returns the data required to get the image and information.
