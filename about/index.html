About this site

When I was younger i was obsessed with magic.

I spent many days studying the likes of David Blane, Penn and teller and Jay Sanky and conducting performances of the same tricks over and over again to hostage family members and friends.

Unlike many, the thrill of magic was not just about creating a magical moment and seeing the effect of the trick I had on the audience but a huge part was about finding out how a trick worked, how I could re-engineer the trick using a different patter and if I could hack tricks together to make a better piece of magic.

Now I have gotten older, I have long since given up on performing magic. It is hard to imagine when I could perform a trick in my daily life developing software or when meeting clients.

I was in Vegas for a conference recently with a colleague/friend of mine and as you do we were drinking and socialising with clients, partners and other friends in the industry. After several whiskeys the colleague asked 'do you want to see some magic?' and produced a deck of cards from his back pocket. Again I felt that excitement and I spent most of the evening watching in awe as he blew the minds of the people he performed to.

While I do not think I have the skills and personality that he had to pull off the spectacular show, I have found the same muse in Development. When you think of the parallels it is almost an obvious correlation. I have swapped the wand for a laptop but the ability to problem solve, investigate how things work and build apps that leave the audience in awe are very much a part of what I do.

No Portfolio

You should create a portfolio! How can a dev not have a portfolio! What if you need to get another job!

These are things i hear all the time, and while i agree with the argument. it just isnt me. My Experience of building applications for businesses of all sizes is evident from my working history and these days most of my 'very serious' code is spent on the product i develop in my working hours.


The purpose of this site was to have an area where i can blog about cool things i find in tech and showcase crazy and sometimes stupid projects that i create when inspiration spikes. The code will be far from production-ready, often scrappy and fairly rushed but always fun!
