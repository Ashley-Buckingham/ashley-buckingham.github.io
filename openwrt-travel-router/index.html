OpenWRT: Travel router

Aug 25, 2022

In March 2020, President Donald Trump made an executive order banning tourists from entering the USA.

After 20 months, in November 2021, President Biden reopened the US, and travel resumed. In May 2022, I finally arrived back in Las Vegas after two years of separation.

While I loved visiting, I was shocked by the tactics some hotels used to scrape back the lost wages from the pandemic—an increase in resort fees, lack of drink service and higher table minimums.

The tactic that affected me the most was limiting devices on the hotel Wi-Fi. I found most hotels would only allow you to connect only one or two devices to Wi-Fi within 24 hours. After that, any additional device would cost 35$ a day!

OpenWRT

I will be travelling back in the coming weeks, so I spent some time researching a solution and found OpenWRT. Using OpenWRT, I could create a small travel router that would enable me to:

  • Connect multiple devices to one connection, removing device limits
  • Use a VPN connection to secure all my internet traffic by default
  • Set up the connection between all my devices to the router once, speeding up the connection time between hotels.

I first decided to look into using a USB WIFI adapter with my Macbook to let me connect my laptop to the Wi-Fi and then share the connection through the external USB Wi-Fi. However, I soon found that support for external Wi-Fi adapters was lacking in the new MacBook pros.

I soon settled on using a raspberry pi, a small credit card-sized computer. Like the Macbook, the Pi has onboard Wi-Fi that I could use to connect to the hotel; I would then use my USB wireless adapter to connect to my other devices. After watching the helpful tutorial by NETWORKCHUCK, I purchased the RT5370 for £7.50.
