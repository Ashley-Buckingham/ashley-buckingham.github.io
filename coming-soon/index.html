About

News • Aug 8, 2022

When I was younger, I was obsessed with magic.

As a child, I was enthralled by magic shows from David Blane, Penn and Teller, and Jay Sankey. I spent hours exploring magic shops in London, searching for the next trick to master and perform for my loved ones.

The excitement of magic lies in the audience's reactions, with TV magicians often leaving people jumping, shouting, and running away in disbelief. But I wasn't interested in performing; I was fascinated by uncovering the secrets behind tricks and reinventing them.

During a recent conference in Vegas, my colleague/friend surprised me with a deck of cards and asked if I wanted to see some magic. The same excitement rushed through me as I spent the evening in awe, watching him astonish groups of clients at the bar.

While I don't see myself becoming a magician, I've discovered a similar passion in development. Magic and software development share the thrill of problem-solving, re-engineering solutions, and creating work that leaves the audience awestruck.

No Portfolio

Create a portfolio! How can a developer not have one? Your resume will look weak without it.

My colleagues ask these questions, and while I understand their point, it's not for me. I've been lucky to work in different businesses with different priorities and technologies.

Lately, I've been focused on my day job as VP of Engineering in the recruitment industry, leaving me little time for at-home "serious" coding. This site is where I share interesting tech stuff and show random projects I make when I feel inspired. The code may not be perfect or ready for production, but it's always fun!
